Critical Aspects of Collaborative Vocabulary Development

Irlán Grangel-González, Lavdim Halilaj, Gökhan Coskun and Sören Auer

Enterprise Information Systems, University of Bonn, Germany

{grangel, halilaj, coskun, auer}@cs.uni-bonn.de


Key words and phrases: Collaborative vocabulary development, collaborative ontology engineering.

Abstract:

A major bottleneck for a wider deployment and use of ontologies and knowledge engineering techniques is the cumbersome and inefficient collaboration support for vocabulary and ontology authoring and curation. We argue, that collaboration and version control techniques (e.g. Git, continuous integration) established for software engineering, can be successfully applied for ontology engineering too. However, successful collaborative vocabulary and ontology engineering requires guidelines and best-practices to organize the collaboration with regard to the aspects documentation, internationalization, naming, structure, reuse and evaluation. In this article, we collect a comprehensive library of guidelines and best-practices. We empirically analyze a number of popular vocabulary and ontology development efforts with regard to their use of the guidelines and best-practices. Inspired by this analysis we proposed a set of practices to address the above mentioned aspects. These practices can improve the communication of the team and, as a result, leverage the development process.
