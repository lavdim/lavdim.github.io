RELATED WORK

Currently, there exist several methodologies for developing ontologies [Pinto et al., 2004,Auer and Herre, 2007,Di Maio, 2011,Suarez-Figueroa et al., 2012,Zeginis et al., 2013]. They covered the main aspects for ontology development with a top-down approach. Specific practices to address how to perform the ontology engineering process regarding the reusing, multilinguality, modularization are still missing.

In [Jansen and Schulz, 2011] are presented 10 principles to follow in ontology engineering. The importance of naming conventions and reuse aspect is recognized in this work. Unfortunately, it neglects to explain how this principles can be applied as a set of specific best practices to follow in vocabulary engineering.

The approach presented in [Poveda-Villalón, 2012] provides a lightweight method for developing Linked Data vocabularies. In this work is presented a workflow that deals with the main tasks for the creation of one ontology. Also, it highlights the reuse of existing terms in the Linked Open Data cloud as a core process in vocabulary building.

[Dodds and Davis, 2011] provides a catalog of best practices for linked data. It contains guidelines for modeling, publishing, and consuming Linked Data. In the two above mentioned works the collaborative aspect of vocabulary development is not addressed.
